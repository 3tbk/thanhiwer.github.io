About Me

I am an adjunct faculty and a postdoc research scholar in the Watershed Sustainability group and Modelling and Assessment of Sustainable Systems (MASS) group at the University of South Florida, USA. I have been a JSPS Fellow and now a collaborator in the Global Hydrodynamics lab at the University of Tokyo, Japan. Before that, I was a research fellow at the Resilient Water Systems group at Singapore University of Technology and Design, Singapore, and a PhD candidate in the Mekong Flows group at the University of Canterbury, New Zealand. I have served as an associate editor for Frontiers in Water, an associate editor for Vietnam Journal of Science, Technology and Engineering (Ministry of Science and Technology, Vietnam), and a Guest Editor for Remote Sensing.

I am working on river hydraulics, water infrastructure design, and several branches of hydrology, including surface hydrology, water resources management, hydroinformatics, water quality, ecohydrology, and hydrogeology. My methodology includes physics-based modeling, remote sensing, optimization, data-driven modeling, forecast-informed operation, geospatial analysis, and data analystics. I am also interested in remote sensing applications in hydrology.
